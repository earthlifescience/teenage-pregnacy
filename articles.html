INTRODUCTION TO TEENAGE PREGNANCY


Pregnancy in a female under the age of 20 is known as teen pregnancy or adolescent pregnancy. Pregnancy can occur after the onset of ovulation, which can occur before the first menstrual period (menarche) but is more commonly after the onset of periods. Teenage pregnancy occurs as a result of parents' lack of discipline with their children, or as a result of teens' increased use of social media and participation in activities that are inappropriate for their age group.

    • IN-DEPTH INFORMATION ABOUT TEENAGE PREGNANCY


    Teen pregnancy is a crisis that affects the infant, the parents, other family members, and community resources. Every year, around 750,000 young women between the ages of 15 and 19 become pregnant. Teen pregnancies pose an added risk to both the infant and the mother's health. Teen pregnancies are more likely to result in low-birth-weight babies and premature deliveries on average. This is due to the fact that many pregnant teenagers do not receive the necessary prenatal care in a timely manner. Furthermore, the aforementioned youngsters are at a higher risk of high blood pressure as a result of their pregnancy and other difficulties.


    Furthermore, teen moms are less likely than other women to complete or attend college, and early marriage is more likely going to result in a divorce. Another potential consequence of teenage pregnancy is abortion. Though formally prohibited in the Philippines, abortion is common in the country. According to Department of Health (2012), 610,000 abortions took place in 2012. Due to its illegal nature, clandestine abortions may carry certain risks, and poorer adolescents may seek out cheaper but riskier methods of abortion.


    According to the 2004 national abortion study, an estimated 22% of poor Filipino women used a catheter or heavy abdominal pressure in an abortion attempt, while no nonpoor women resorted to such methods. Moreover, poor women were far less likely to use safer methods, such as dilation and curettage or manual vacuum aspiration (MVA), than the nonpoor (13% vs. 55%). About 1,000 Filipino women die each year from abortion complications, which contributes to the nation’s high maternal mortality rate. Projections that were based on data from 2000 indicate that 100,000 women were hospitalized for abortion complications in 2012; countless others suffered complications that went untreated.


    Teenage pregnancy can also lead to child abandonment. According to research done by Kaiman J, et. Al.(n.d.), it is estimated that nearly 2 million children in the Philippines are either neglected or abandoned by their households as a result of social, environmental, and economic factors. These high numbers of abandoned children can be attributed to violent conflict as a result of the country’s stance on drugs, mothers forced to find domestic work overseas, high levels of poverty and teen pregnancy, and particular natural disasters, all culminating in children being taken in by orphanages.


    HOW CAN WE STOP THIS PROBLEM?

    SOLUTIONS FOR TEENAGE PREGNANCY


    Teenage Pregnancy is one of the most important concerns that Filipino youth are facing today. For mothers and their children, teen childbearing can have health, economic, and social consequences. Teens frequently utilize condom and birth control tablets, which, when not used consistently and appropriately, are less effective at preventing pregnancy. Below are the many ways of preventing teenage pregnancy:


    - Educating young generation regarding sexual and reproductive health, even in non-formal settings, and normalizing respectful discussion on the subject.


    - Implementing protections programs to support pregnant girls and young mothers in continuing and completing their education


    - Ensuring that anti-violence against women and children workstations in barangays are operational.


    - By practicing sexual abstinence.


    - Providing a comprehensive sex Education to the youth. Through this, it'll expand their knowledge in terms of sexual engagements.


    - Providing free contraception (condoms, birth control pills, IUDs).


    - By being aware of the consequences when practicing any sexual activities.


    - Providing counseling and regular check-ups for the health of those who are affected.


    - Providing materials / safe place for teenage parents if ever they are having a hard time with their baby.
